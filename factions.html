The Crown
The Crown is the ruling cast of the Kingdom of Erie. When the crown is mentioned, it means the 6 Noble Houses that control the country. While House Pendragon is unquestioningly the ruling body, where the King and his extended family, each House has their own economic, military, political, and magical might, with each holding governing territory across the whole of the Kingdom. While House Pendragon can function as the ruling entity for Kingdom, each other House has been purposefully set up in such a way that they rely on the other Houses. One of the decrees issued by the First King, so that the others may not rise up and challenge the rightful king. Government is set up that each Noble House holds seats and representatives in a council called the Deliberative, where the King or his designates make the final decision. (edited)
HOUSE PENDRAGON The ruling family unit that holds a main branch and dozens of branch families. Each branch is meant to govern the larger cities of Erie. Humans, Elves, Dwarves, even Gnomes have been formerly married into the family and govern the cities that house the primary members of their race. It is also known that the blood of dragons runs deep within the family. Many of them are acclaimed sorcerors, and proudly show off their wings and scales, to commemorate the Dragon that helped birth their branch family. Indeed, the King can even call upon the Dragons themselves to do his bidding, ensuring the continued survival of the Kingdom.
(edited)
HOUSE GILDER The Banking system. Its Gilder that manages all of the coin and taxes that run through the Kingdom and assure that trade is obscured. They can offer loans and lines of credit to anyone and everyone, and hold an army of debt collectors and entire archives of those who owe them favors. House Gilder never forgets, and commonly uses The Temple as a way to leverage someone, physically, magically, emotionally, or spiritually. Without House Gilder, the finances of the other Houses and their lands will fall into destitution.
(edited)
HOUSE CANDOR The Industrial system. This house works primarily in production. Most farming towns and villages are owned by House Candor, and thus the House holds a special place in the hearts of the common man, and often do speak for social rights. House Candor also sponsors shipwrights, logging camps, guilds, artisans, and tradesman to produce the goods the company needs. Its liable that if House Candor fails, most of the country would starve.
(edited)
HOUSE FEATHER The Entertainment system. With more tools and inventions, the common folk have begun to find more time to themselves. House Feather capitalized on that fact. Taverns, Brothels, Stage Shows, Gladiatorial Bouts, Newpapers, Criers, Magical Tournaments, Plays, Fashion, Makeup, and Music of all kinds are being fashioned each and every day to amuse the populace. By far the most favored house, and incidentally one that controls information. Their media outlets can spin the truth, their prostitutes now such things from their patrons. Distraction and Information are their goods and trade. They would very much like to Destroy the Church of Ebony and Satin for taking their business. Without House Feathers, the other Houses are liable to have commoner revolts on their hands. Boredom leads to thoughts after all.
(edited)
HOUSE TALON The Military system. House Talon is the standing military and armada not owned by the personal legions of House Pendragon. They maintain order within the kingdom and fight those without, whether they be man, beast, or monster. They maintain the skyship fleets and Sea Armadas and the legions that are the sword, shield, and spear of Erie. They commonly are hired by other houses and organizations as mercenaries, and employ quite a few themselves. Without them, the other Houses and their lands are defenseless.
(edited)
HOUSE MANCER The Infrastructural System. A kingdom needs buildings, roads, towers, walls. It needs furniture, tools, factories to make them. It needs engineers, specialists, laborers. It needs hardwork and lots of hands. Men, women, children, beastkin. It doesn't matter. Erie is a country moving to become one of the greatest empires on Elysia, and it rides upon the backs of its loyal citizen towards a new shining future. Without House Mancer, the other houses do not have the manufactories or engineers to build, maintain, and repair.
(edited)

The Temple
Despite the ascension of Lady Sune, the Pantheon of Erie is vast, vast and active, and thus the people worship them. 95% of the population atleast mouth a few words of respect, requesting the favor of the gods of hearth, bounty, wealth, safety, and luck. Just as often a citizen will pray for a god NOT to do something, avoiding misfortune, or curses. On holy days, its considered in good form to sacrifice a little to the gods that oversee your job, your town, your family. There is alot of money to be had in worship and the Temple controls all of it.
The Temple acts as the Religious Authority for the Kingdom of Erie. Its Monks, Paladins, and Clerics act as an umbrella for all other cults, temples, and churches. For tithes they allow them to worship as they please in whatever way serves the gods. If they don't pay, the Temple will speak against their 'lack of faith' and worship becomes more and more difficult. They still bribe the gods whom are losing followers, but just as often they funnel prayers into rival, more lucrative spirits, at the expense of the weaker cheaper ones. After all, what do you need money for if you want to go to heaven?
They have control over alot of social interactions, and citizens can go to them to buy prayers, and benedictions or health. Still they are not completely heartless as they front many orphanages, poor houses, and social welfare programs. How pious and merciful the temple is! Be sure to remember who helped you when no one else would...
